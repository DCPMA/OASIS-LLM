## The original OASIS procedure

Kurdi, Lozano, &amp; Banaji (2017) normed 900 open-access color images on two affective dimensions:

- **Valence** — the level of positivity vs. negativity intrinsic to the image.
- **Arousal** — the level of intensity vs. calmness intrinsic to the image, treated as orthogonal to valence.

Both dimensions were rated on a **7-point Likert scale** with verbal anchors at every step.

### Sample structure (paper)

- 822 usable MTurk participants, restricted to ≥90% approval and ≥50 prior HITs.
- Images split into **4 lists of 225 images** each. Each participant saw one list, in individually randomized order.
- **Between-subject** assignment: each participant rated **only one** dimension (valence _or_ arousal), never both.
- ~100 ratings per (image, dimension) cell after balancing across lists.

## The OASIS-LLM replication

We replay the rating procedure against an LLM, holding the _instructions_ constant and varying only the rater.

### What we keep verbatim

- The 7-point scale, with the paper's exact anchor labels for each dimension.
- The image-centered instruction wording (the paper's main study used this variant only — internal-state-focused instructions were used only for a methodological appendix).
- The instruction to rate arousal **independently of valence**.

The system prompts are loaded directly from `src/oasis_llm/prompts.py`:

### What we change

<Warning>
  Two design choices in the LLM replication differ deliberately from the human
  protocol. They make the replication cheaper and simpler, but they limit what
  we can claim by direct comparison. See [Discrepancies](/discrepancies) for the
  full list and rationale.
</Warning>

**Within-subject instead of between-subject.** A single model run rates _both_ valence and arousal for every image. The paper kept these strictly between-subject so that one rating could not anchor the other. We collapse them to one run because the per-trial cost is dominated by the image upload, and because we want both columns out of every model with one config.

**Smaller N per cell.** The default is `samples_per_image: 5`, giving 5 ratings per (image, dimension) cell instead of the paper's ~100. This is a deliberate cost/reliability trade. By the Spearman-Brown prophecy, if the paper-level inter-rater reliability is ρ ≈ 0.984 for valence at k = 102 raters, the expected reliability at k = 5 raters is

$$
\rho_{k'} = \frac{(k'/k) \cdot \rho}{1 + ((k'/k) - 1) \cdot \rho} \approx 0.762
$$

That is the ceiling of what 5 model samples can achieve as a cell-mean estimate, _assuming the model ratings have the same true between-image variance and noise structure as humans_. They almost certainly don't, so treat this number as a budgeting tool, not a guarantee.

`samples_per_image` is excluded from the run's `canonical_hash`, so you can resume a run later with a higher `samples_per_image` and the harness will only enqueue the _new_ sample indices.

### Trial structure

Each trial is uniquely identified by:

```text
(run_id, image_id, dimension, sample_idx)
```

- `run_id` — the `name` field from the YAML config.
- `image_id` — the OASIS filename stem (e.g. `Alarm clock 1`).
- `dimension` — `"valence"` or `"arousal"`.
- `sample_idx` — `0..samples_per_image-1`. With `cache_buster: true` (the default), each `sample_idx` produces a different per-trial salt that is appended to the user prompt to force decoding-path divergence at temperature=0. See [Cache buster](/cache-buster).

The full trial table schema is in [Workflow](/workflow#trial-record).
